# Mutton Vindaloo Recipe

### Ingredients:

- **For the Mutton**:
  - 750g mutton
  - 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  - 1 tsp salt
  - 1 onion, sliced
  - A handful of fresh coriander
  - 2 medium-sized potatoes, peeled and chopped

- **For the Spice Paste**:
  - 20 garlic cloves (1-2 garlic)
  - 2 1/2 inches of ginger
  - 3 tsp cumin seeds
  - A pinch of salt

- **For the Gravy**:
  - 2 1/2 tomatoes, ground into a paste
  - 4 tbsp freshly grated coconut (ground into a smooth paste, like butter)

- **For Seasoning**:
  - 1 onion, chopped
  - 2 green chilies, slit
  - 4 tbsp oil
  - 2 tbsp chili powder (adjust to taste)
  - 1/2 tsp salt (adjust to taste)

---

### Instructions:

#### 1. Prepare the Mutton:
1. Wash the mutton thoroughly.  
   ![Placeholder image for washing mutton](path/to/placeholder-image1.jpg)
2. Place the mutton in a vessel and heat until all the water evaporates.  
   ![Placeholder image for cooking mutton](path/to/placeholder-image2.jpg)
3. Add enough hot water to cover the mutton completely.
4. Add 1/2 tsp turmeric powder and 1 tsp salt. Stir well and cover the vessel.  
   ![Placeholder image for adding turmeric and salt](path/to/placeholder-image3.jpg)
5. Add 1 sliced onion and a handful of fresh coriander.
6. Transfer to a pressure cooker and cook for 8 whistles:  
   - Start on high heat, then lower the flame after the first whistle.  
   ![Placeholder image for pressure cooking](path/to/placeholder-image4.jpg)
7. Once 95% cooked, add the chopped potatoes and set aside.  
   ![Placeholder image for adding potatoes](path/to/placeholder-image5.jpg)

#### 2. Prepare the Spice Paste:
1. Combine 20 garlic pods, 2 1/2 inches of ginger, 3 tsp cumin seeds, and a pinch of salt.  
   ![Placeholder image for spice paste ingredients](path/to/placeholder-image6.jpg)
2. Grind to a smooth paste using a mixer.  
   ![Placeholder image for grinding spice paste](path/to/placeholder-image7.jpg)

#### 3. Prepare the Coconut and Tomato Paste:
1. Grind 2 1/2 tomatoes into a smooth paste.  
   ![Placeholder image for grinding tomatoes](path/to/placeholder-image8.jpg)
2. Grind 4 tbsp of coconut until it becomes a smooth, buttery consistency.  
   ![Placeholder image for grinding coconut](path/to/placeholder-image9.jpg)

#### 4. Seasoning and Cooking the Gravy:
1. Heat 4 tbsp oil in a pot over medium heat.  
   ![Placeholder image for heating oil](path/to/placeholder-image10.jpg)
2. Add 2 slit green chilies and 1 chopped onion. Fry until the onion is golden and cooked.  
   ![Placeholder image for frying onions and chilies](path/to/placeholder-image11.jpg)
3. Add the cumin, ginger, and garlic paste. Lower the heat and fry.  
   - Add 2 tbsp chili powder and cook until the raw smell is gone.  
   ![Placeholder image for cooking spice paste](path/to/placeholder-image12.jpg)
4. Add the tomato paste and 1/2 tsp salt. Increase the flame to medium and adjust the seasoning as needed.  
   ![Placeholder image for adding tomato paste](path/to/placeholder-image13.jpg)
5. Stir in the coconut paste and cook on low heat until the gravy thickens and the flavors meld.  
   ![Placeholder image for adding coconut paste](path/to/placeholder-image14.jpg)

#### 5. Combine and Serve:
1. Add the cooked mutton and potatoes to the gravy.  
   ![Placeholder image for combining mutton with gravy](path/to/placeholder-image15.jpg)
2. Simmer on low heat for a few minutes until everything is well combined and heated through.  
   ![Placeholder image for simmering mutton vindaloo](path/to/placeholder-image16.jpg)
3. Garnish with fresh coriander and serve hot with steamed rice or naan.  
   ![Placeholder image for final dish](path/to/placeholder-image17.jpg)

---

### Notes:
- Adjust the chili powder and salt to taste based on your spice preference.
- For a richer flavor, you can use ghee instead of oil for frying.
